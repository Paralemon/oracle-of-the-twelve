Oracle of the Twelve

What it is

The Oracle of the Twelve is a divination by three twelve-sided dice. The first carries the luminaries, planets, and lunar nodes — Sun, Moon, Mercury, Venus, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune, Pluto, and Rahu and Ketu, the north and south nodes. The second carries the twelve signs of the zodiac. The third carries the twelve houses of the natal chart. Cast together, they speak as a single placement.

The reading

Every placement is three layers braided into one.

The planet is the what — the force at work. The sign is the how — the temperament it wears. The house is the where — the field of life in which it moves.

A cast of Saturn in Scorpio in the 8th, for instance, reads as enduring discipline (Saturn) expressed through unflinching depth (Scorpio) in the chamber of intimacy, shared resources, and transformation (the 8th house).

The lineage

The system stands on three older feet. From horary and electional astrology, the principle that the heavens at the moment of a question contain something of the question's answer. From cleromancy — the lot-casting of the ancient Mediterranean, of the Yoruba diviners, of the I Ching — the principle that chance, approached attentively, is one of the languages the world uses to speak. From the natal chart, the twelvefold map of a life: the planets that move, the signs that color, the houses that locate.

A thrown placement is a small horary chart in miniature. The dice freeze the heavens at the moment of your asking.

How to use it well

Approach with a real question, even an unspoken one. Hold the question in mind as you shake the dice. Cast once and accept the cast — let the placement work on you across the day rather than across the instant.

The first reading speaks to the placement in general — its archetypal note. If a particular thread calls — what does this mean for me right now?, how should I act on this? — ask the oracle directly. The live response will answer in your specific frame, anchored in the placement you were given.

Twelve planets. Twelve signs. Twelve houses. One thousand seven hundred twenty-eight castings — each a mirror with a different facet turned toward you.
